Wikipedia Shortest Path

November 2015 - present

As an adaptation to Godwin's Law and a final project for my Data Structures course, David Newswanger and I took on the challenge of indexing all of Wikipedia to find the shortest path between two articles by following the Wikipedia links within that article. The project taught me to deal with very large files and efficiently index and store information from the file. This was my first experience with a massive graph theory problem that was made even more difficult by the limited memory and processing power of a Dell laptop.

EPG Website

July 2015 - August 2015

I worked as a designer and front-end developer in building a professional commercial web application for the Entrepreneurship for the Public Good program at Berea College. I was in charge of taking customer preferences and turning them into wire-frames, mockups, and finally the finished product. I used AJAX to retrieve profiles from a database and search them on the client side. This was an invaluable first experience in developing on a commercial scale and helped me to learn to make my designs universal and minimize server load by keeping calculations on the client side.

BluLocker

April 2015 - present (suspended)

As part of a final project for my Software Design and Implementation course I embarked on BluLocker. BluLocker is a PC based application that automatically locks the workstation when a paired Blutooth device goes out of range. If you pair something that you typically keep on your person (phone or wearable), this effectively locks your computer when you leave it. BluLocker was written in Python and I currently have a minimum viable product... but there is much to be desired. I will likely make a GitHub repo for it in the near future.
